The Sloth

Metamorphic Perching Quadrotor

"The Sloth" is a venture into adopting an animal's behavioral strategy into the design and mission of a robot. The study of animal biomechanics has spawned many capable mechanical designs, but the strategy each animal deploys to thrive in their environment is equally significant. I combined a power-hungry quadcopter with the sloth's unwillingness to move to imagine what a lazy ultra-energy-conservative flying machine should be.

The core innovation from the Sloth project is the morphing flying-perching quadcopter arm. The technical challenge of this robot arm is to maintain airworthiness while maximizing perching performance and perching energy efficiency. Reducing the weight of the airframe helps to perch more securely but decreases the airworthiness as the robot becomes more flexible. Using a stronger/larger servomotor to drive the perching mechanism improves its griping strength but the robot becomes heavier and uses more energy to stay perched.
